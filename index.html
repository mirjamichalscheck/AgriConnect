The Problem

In 2025, we interviewed 1,200 smallholder farmers in Kenya and Uganda. They told us that "lack of information" was the main obstacle preventing them from adopting more sustainable farming practices — consistent with findings by Ndambi et al. (2019) and Owino et al. (2020).

Governmental extension services are understaffed and reach only a fraction of those who need advice. Much information already exists — the challenge is getting it to farmers.

Our Solution

We meet farmers where they are — on their phones — with trusted, science-backed content in languages they speak, through devices they already own.

📱 Kenya
WhatsApp Chatbot

An AI-driven chatbot in English and Kiswahili, trained exclusively on fact-checked content curated by Agroscope and CGIAR scientists. Built for high smartphone ownership in Kenya, with an SMS dial-in option so feature phone users can also participate — making it a socially inclusive solution. Smartphone users additionally receive multimedia content: videos and graphical manuals for sustainable farm management.

📞 Uganda
Voice Hotline (IVR)

In Uganda, where smartphone ownership is lower, we offer Interactive Voice Response (IVR) — a hotline farmers call to access pre-recorded content in their preferred local language. No smartphone needed, no data costs. Your content, delivered by voice.

About Us

We are a partnership of organisations that share a passion for impact — bringing science to the people who need it most, with a special focus on women and youth farmers.

Our team brings together expertise in ICT for development, agricultural science, livestock research, and farmer community engagement across East Africa.

Viamo — Social enterprise building ICT solutions for development
Agroscope — Switzerland's federal agricultural research station
ILRI / CGIAR — International Livestock Research Institute, Kenya & Uganda
WoFaAK — Women Farmers Association of Kenya
